Burst-to-Queue Ledger

A two-database architecture that absorbs traffic spikes your production database can't.

Production databases weren't built for flash mobs.

When 10,000 fans hit "Buy" at the same moment, your transactional database faces a wall of concurrent writes. Rows lock. Connections saturate. Users get errors — or worse, inconsistent state.

At 500 concurrent users, a direct database takes 3.4s p95 and drops 1 in 10 requests.
